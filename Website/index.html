Visible Light Communication



In recent years, Visible Light Communication (VLC) has emerged as a complementary technique to overcome limitations of the crowded radio frequency (RF) spectrum. Its superior characteristics include unlicensed wide bandwidth, high security and dual-use nature (Illumination and data). Our prototype transmits data using LEDs and receives the same using a photodetector. To implement this VLC link, we have utilized National Instruments LabVIEW with Ettus USRP (Universal Software Radio Peripheral) N210, and an optical transmitter and photosensitive receiver as a replacement for conventional RF antennas.

Overview



LEDs (LUW CN5M) transmit binary data to the receiver photodetector (PDA36A). The intermediate components are Bias Tee (ZX85-12G+) and SMA Cables. USRPs (N210) are used for transmission and reception of data.

LabVIEW VI provides Transmitter and Receiver Block sets for audio transmission through Visible Light.

Working



Block Diagram

This is the set up that we created to demonstrate the data transfer using Visible Light. On the transmitter PC, there is a LabVIEW VI which decodes a sound file, modulates it using Frequency Modulation with a 1 MHz carrier frequency and transmitter gain of 10dB. As the USRP is N210, the USRPs and PCs are connected through a local network.
The transmitter USRP is connected to the bias tee using an SMA cable. The bias tee converts the bipolar signal generated by the USRP with a 2V DC bias which is provided by the voltage source such that the input to the LED driver is within the linear range of conversion. The bias tee gives output to the PCB which has ultra white colored LEDs and driver circuit containing MOSFETs.
The receiver hardware is a photo-detector with a trans-impedance amplifier circuit which converts the optical signal back to analog. On PC 2, the LabVIEW VI receives the analog signal and encodes it into a sound file which is played continuously.


Hardware

  • USRP N210 for both Transmitter and Receiver side, with LFTX and LFRX daughterboards respectively. N210 Ettus
  • LEDs-LUWCN5M (ultra white) were used for transmission. LED LUWCN5M
  • MOSFETs 2N7002 are used to drive the LEDs in the transmitter PCB. LED Driver MOSFET
  • Bias T ZX85-12G+ is used to provide linear DC bias to PCB. Bias TEE
  • At the receiver end, photodetector PDA36A with trans-impedence amplifier circuit is used. Photodetector

Impact



Visible Light Communication enables data transmission using LEDs for shorter distances. It has found its applications in indoor environments. Visible Light Communication has found its place mainly to overcome the limitations of RF Spectrum, VLC is cheaper to implement and also, this technology can change the thought of not wasting light (electricity) but using light for a purpose (data transmission in our case).

  • Street lights can transmit information to a vehicle if there is an accident further along the road or the GPS co-ordinates of the location.
  • Lights in colleges can transmit critical information to all the devices.
  • Looking at the employability space, this new technology can provide work to people.
  • The VLC can be used to transfer data from one mobile device to another with inherent security.
